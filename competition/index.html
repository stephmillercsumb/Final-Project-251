Yo-Yo Competitions

The world’s first yo-yo competition dates back to 1932 in London, England. Today, yo-yo competitions take place on local and global levels. The International Yo-Yo Federation is the leading organization that hosts and oversees global yo-yo competitions. “The International Yo-Yo Federation is a nonprofit organization dedicated to developing and promoting yo-yoing as a sport on a global level...We know that yo-yo is mainly a toy and hobby, but we believe it is also amazing sports equipment with a great competition scene and huge potential. We will aim to keep this scene and yo-yo community easy to join, and friendly and enjoyed by all people regardless of gender, ethnic background, faith or culture.” Check out link below to see the 2019’s yo-yo contest winners.

Contest Divisions

1A: String tricks with a single yo-yo. This is what is typically thought of when a yo-yo competition comes into mind.





Video Link

2A: The use of two looping yo-yos, one in each hand. High-level AA play consists of various series of continuous regenerations, such as loops, hops, moons, punches, stalls, etc.



Video Link

3A: String (A.K.A. Long Spin) tricks with two yo-yos. This division involves the use of a yo-yo in each hand and performing trick with them together.




Video Link

4A: Where the yo-yo is not attached to the string, but the string is tied to the finger. A variation of this is known as Chinese Yo-yoing.




Video Link

5A: Where a weight (a casino die or small ball) is attached to the end of the yo-yo string that would otherwise be attached to the finger. The yo-yo itself is attached to the string in the normal manner.



Video Link

Artistic Performance: A freestyle yo-yoing that uses any type of yo-yo or other prop in order to perform an artistic routine. This style is based mostly on performance, this is where theater meets yo-yo.
